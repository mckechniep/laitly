Laitly — Project Brief for LLM Recreation Laitly is a mobile app for breastfeeding mothers that eliminates the mental math of milk storage, tracking, and planning. The name is a play on "lait" (French for milk). The brand color is called colostrum gold (#C5A55A-ish). The tagline: "We carry the details. You carry the baby." The problem it solves: 1 in 3 breastfeeding mothers stop earlier than intended due to logistical overwhelm — not desire. Mothers are counting oz by hand from memory, guessing expiry dates, calculating coverage at 3am, and getting no clear signal on whether to pump again before leaving. Laitly does that math for them. Mission statement: Laitly takes the mental load of breastfeeding off a mother's plate — so she can just be present. It does the milk math, tracks movement, protects supply, and plans for moments away. Core design philosophy: - No output judgement — Oz are logged, never evaluated. No benchmarks, no comparisons, no deficits shown. - Me = formula engine — Baby's bottle size × daily feeds = every calculation. One source of truth. - Auto-populate everything — Movement tab pre-fills from last session. Logging friction near zero. - Partner view is simplified — Shows only what a caregiver needs: oz ready, instructions, log button. - LTD counter — Lifetime oz produced on the home screen. Celebrates the mother's effort over time. App Architecture — 5 tabs 1. Home — LTD oz produced, time as a mom, breastfeeding streak, reminders, quick access. Celebrates her effort and alerts her to what needs attention. Has two views: mother view and partner view. 2. Movement — Add milk / Move milk / Used milk. Three states, one screen, auto-populated from last entry. Fastest possible interaction — 10 seconds to log. 3. Lait — Total oz stored. Fridge vs freezer breakdown. Coverage in oz, bottles, or days (toggle). Expiry tracking. The core milk math tab. 4. Guides — AAP guidance, FAQs, how-to content, storage tips, pump reviews, pitcher method, milk bricks. The trust layer — answers questions before she Googles them. Categories: Storage, Feeding, AAP. 5. Me — Baby's bottle size, daily intake, stash goal, breastfeeding goal. Strategy (return-to-work, trips, hours away). Partner access. Reminders. Settings. The formula engine that powers every calculation. Visual Design Language - Palette: Warm cream/off-white backgrounds, colostrum gold (#C5A55A-ish) for accents, progress bars, active states, and highlights. Dark brown/charcoal for primary text and filled buttons. - Typography: Serif font for the app name "Lait" on the Lait tab. Small-caps with wide letter-spacing for section headers (e.g., "TOTAL STORED", "WHERE IT LIVES", "MY BABY", "STRATEGY", "REMINDERS"). Clean sans-serif for body text. - Components: Guide cards have a left gold border accent. Progress bars use the gold fill. Toggle buttons use dark fill for selected state, outline for unselected. Dot indicators (yellow for urgent/fridge, dark for other) on list items. Primary CTAs are full-width dark/black buttons. - Tone: Calm, editorial, intentionally non-clinical. Warm and supportive, never sterile or medical-feeling.
