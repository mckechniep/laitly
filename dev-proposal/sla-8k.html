02Project Overview

The Developer agrees to design, develop, and deliver Laitly, a cross-platform mobile application (iOS & Android) that helps breastfeeding mothers track milk inventory, plan for time away, and reduce the mental load of breastfeeding logistics.

This Agreement covers the full v1.0.0 scope as defined in the accompanying Development Proposal dated March 2026.

03Scope of Work

The v1.0.0 deliverable includes the following features and functionality:

  • Milk Movement — Logging of every pump, bottle feed, and storage transfer
  • Milk Math (Lait Tab) — Real-time inventory with oz / bottle / days toggle across fridge and freezer
  • Fridge + Freezer Inventory Tracking — Storage location management with expiry alerts and notifications
  • Baby Intake Profile — Configurable intake data to power calculations and strategy
  • Return-to-Work & Trip Strategy Planner — Oz targets based on baby's intake and time away
  • Home Dashboard — Overview with lifetime counter (LTD)
  • Partner / Caregiver View — Shared access for caregivers
  • Guides & FAQs — AAP-backed content and best practices
  • User Profile (Me Tab) — Profile, goals, reminders, and access management
  • iOS + Android Deployment — Cross-platform build, tested and published

Any features, integrations, or functionality not explicitly listed above are considered out of scope and may be addressed in future phases at additional cost upon mutual agreement.

04Timeline & Milestones

Development will follow a sequential build model over 16 weeks, organized into four milestones:

Milestone Timeline Deliverables
Discovery & Design Weeks 1–4 Brand alignment, design system, architecture, database schema, dev environment
Core Engine Weeks 5–8 Movement tab, Lait tab, storage logic, calculations, expiry tracking
Full Build Weeks 9–12 Home dashboard, Guides, Me + Strategy tabs, partner view, notifications
Launch & Deploy Weeks 13–16 QA & testing, iOS deploy, Android deploy, performance, final polish

Timeline assumes timely feedback and approvals from Client at each milestone. Delays in client response may extend the timeline proportionally.
